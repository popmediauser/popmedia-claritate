# Pop Media Innovative Promotion Product Research

Date: 2026-06-24
Scope: product idea for Romanian/community promotion in Flanders/Belgium. No outreach, no public launch, no page changes in this pass.

## Executive Conclusion

The strongest product for Pop Media is:

**Vocea Afacerii Locale**

One-line definition:

> A promotion package that turns a local business into an audio story, a shareable QR page, social content, and a simple visibility report for businesses that want credible Romanian-language promotion in Belgium.

Internal names that can also work:

- `Pachet Promo Local Romania-Belgia`
- `Community Spotlight Pack`

This should not be sold as a generic ad package. It should be sold as a trusted community visibility product: voice, story, local context, and reusable assets.

## Why This Is The Best Opportunity

### 1. The audience exists

Flanders has a meaningful Romanian community signal. Statistics Flanders says Romanians are among the largest non-Belgian nationality groups in the Flemish Region, after Dutch nationals. It also says people with Romanian nationality were the largest group in total international immigration to the Flemish Region in 2024.

This does not prove Pop Media can reach everyone. It does prove the niche is real enough to build a focused media product around it.

### 2. Generic digital promotion is crowded

Belgian businesses already use websites and social networks heavily. Statbel reports that 9 out of 10 enterprises have a website and 4 out of 5 use social networks.

So the unique offer should not be "we post on social media". That is too common.

The unique offer is:

- Romanian-language trust;
- human voice;
- interview/story format;
- local community context;
- radio/podcast/social/event reuse;
- a simple page that can be shared by QR, WhatsApp, Facebook, or email;
- a report simple enough for a small business owner to understand.

### 3. Audio and host-read formats are naturally trust-based

Podcast/audio sponsorships often work because the message is read or framed by a trusted voice, not just pushed as a cold banner. Acast describes host-read sponsorships as longer commercial messages delivered in the host's own voice and style, usually around 60 seconds to a few minutes.

Pop Media can adapt this idea for Romanian-speaking businesses in Belgium, without needing a complex platform.

### 4. Static-first is enough for the first version

The first version can stay simple:

- a static sponsor/story page;
- one audio file or embedded player;
- a QR code;
- manual publishing;
- manual report.

No dashboard, no login, no database, no paid automation is needed for the pilot.

## Product Shape

### Core Offer

`Vocea Afacerii Locale` is a paid package for businesses that want to be discovered and trusted by Romanian-speaking people in Belgium.

The package creates one clear story around the business:

- who they are;
- what problem they solve;
- who they help;
- where they operate;
- how people contact them;
- why the community should trust them.

## MVP Deliverables

For one sponsor:

1. 20-minute intake call or questionnaire.
2. Message polish: headline, short offer, trust angle.
3. 3-5 minute interview, story, or audio feature.
4. 20-30 second audio spot.
5. Static QR story page with:
   - short Romanian presentation;
   - offer;
   - audio;
   - contact buttons;
   - location/service area;
   - sponsor label;
   - optional short NL/FR summary.
6. 2 social posts or short reels.
7. Radio/podcast mention when available.
8. 7/14/30-day visibility report with simple numbers:
   - page visits;
   - QR scans, if tracked;
   - contact clicks;
   - social reach/engagement;
   - sponsor feedback or reported leads.

## Why It Can Feel Unique

This is not just a directory listing.

This is not just a Facebook post.

This is not just a radio ad.

This is a small media product that combines:

- voice;
- story;
- Romanian-language clarity;
- local credibility;
- offline-to-online bridge through QR;
- reusable content;
- transparent sponsored placement;
- simple proof after the campaign.

That combination is the product.

## First Buyer Personas

Best first targets:

- accountants, fiscal consultants, legal and insurance services;
- construction, renovation, cleaning, and local trades;
- transport, parcels, auto services, and driving schools;
- Romanian/Balkan restaurants, shops, and event venues;
- event organizers and cultural/community partners;
- recruiters or training providers, only if claims are handled carefully.

Why these categories:

- trust matters before purchase;
- the service often needs explanation;
- Romanian-language clarity can reduce friction;
- the buyer can value a warm community context more than generic clicks.

## Pricing Hypothesis

Do not sell CPM or big audience promises yet. Sell the deliverables and the trust context.

Pilot pricing:

- Founder pilot: `250-350 EUR` for the first 3 sponsors, in exchange for feedback and permission to use anonymized results.
- Standard package: `500 EUR`.
- Plus package: `900-1,200 EUR`.
- Event/partner sponsor: `1,500+ EUR`, depending on event presence and deliverables.

Suggested tiers:

| Tier | Fit | Includes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Start | Small business testing visibility | QR page, 20-30s audio spot, 1 social post |
| Spotlight | Main recommended package | Interview/story, audio spot, 2 posts/reels, report |
| Event / Partner | Events and recurring partners | Spotlight plus event/radio/podcast presence and recap |

## 30-Day Validation Plan

### Days 1-5: Package And Demo

- Create a one-page offer.
- Create a simple rate card.
- Create one fictional/demo sponsor story page.
- Define report metrics.
- Prepare sponsor intake questions.

### Days 6-10: Target List

- Build a private list of 25 possible sponsors.
- Group by category and city/region.
- Draft short outreach messages.

No outreach should be sent without Marius approval.

### Days 11-20: Pilot Conversations

After approval:

- talk to 10-15 potential sponsors;
- aim for 2 paid pilots;
- ask what deliverables they understand and what they find confusing;
- refine the package name and price.

### Days 21-30: Publish And Learn

- publish 1-2 pilot pages;
- distribute through Pop Media channels;
- collect simple numbers;
- send a short sponsor report;
- decide whether to keep, rename, or change the package.

## Compliance And Trust Rules

This product should be transparent from day one.

Use clear labels:

- `Publicitate`
- `Promovare platita`
- `Material realizat in colaborare cu...`

Do not rely only on vague words like `parteneriat`, because official guidance around influencer/commercial content says advertising must be immediately recognizable.

If AI is used:

- do not hide synthetic voices or images;
- get sponsor approval before publishing;
- label AI-generated or AI-assisted synthetic content when it could mislead;
- prefer a real human voice for trust-sensitive sponsors.

Avoid:

- guaranteed leads;
- "ajungem la toti romanii din Belgia";
- "numarul 1" without proof;
- fake testimonials;
- fake audience numbers;
- ethnic ad targeting claims on platforms.

Safe public wording:

> Promovare in limba romana, in contexte de comunitate, pentru afaceri care vor sa fie intelese si tinute minte.

## What To Build First On The Website

Only after the offer is approved:

1. A small `Pentru afaceri` section focused on `Vocea Afacerii Locale`.
2. Three clear deliverable blocks:
   - `Poveste audio`
   - `Pagina QR`
   - `Raport simplu`
3. One CTA:
   - `Cere pachetul`
4. One example/mock sponsor card.

Do not add a noisy platform dashboard or many service boxes yet.

## Product Positioning Copy

Short paragraph:

> Transformam mesajul afacerii tale intr-o poveste audio, o pagina usor de distribuit si continut pentru social media, cu o prezentare clara in limba romana si un raport simplu dupa campanie.

Sponsor explanation:

## Decision

Recommended next step:

Create a demo version of `Vocea Afacerii Locale` using a fictional sponsor, then add only a small website section if the demo feels clear.

This keeps Pop Media static-first, avoids noise, and creates a product that can be sold before building a backend.
